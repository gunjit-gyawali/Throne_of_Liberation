A long time ago, the world wasn’t whole—but it was in harmony. Six great lands floated above an endless sea, massive islands suspended in the sky like drifting continents.

They weren’t named by where they sat in the world, but by what they were—each one holding a piece of existence itself.

The Land of the Present: vibrant, bustling, the heart of culture, trade, and balance.

The Land of the Past: steeped in tradition, cloaked in ancient history and long-forgotten wisdom.

The Land of the Future: a place of progress and invention, where ambition pushed the edge of what was possible.

The Land of Heaven: serene and still, closest to the sky, where spirits whispered and prayers carried weight.

The Land of Hell: wild, untamed, a raw surge of chaos and magic unbound.

The Land of Power: the core of strength, where warriors and mages of legend were born—and where greatness was forged.

In this world, magic wasn’t rare. It was life. It flowed through the air like wind, shimmered in the water, flickered in fire.

And above them all stood the Archons—six rulers, each the embodiment of their land’s essence. They were more than leaders. They were guardians. Symbols of peace. Anchors of balance.

For centuries, the lands coexisted. Separate, but connected. Different, but united.

Until the sky bled.

Fifteen years ago, it came without warning. Meteors, massive and burning, tore through the clouds like falling gods. One struck each land.

From their craters crawled things that looked like humans—but weren’t. They wore stolen skin like costumes. But their blood was black. Their eyes hollow. Their magic, wrong.

And with each meteor came a commander. A creature of nightmares, leading armies of star-born monsters.

The Archons fought. But one by one, they fell.

Until only one remained—the Land of Power.

There, the final Archon made his stand. He fought for twenty days and twenty nights. In the end, he gave everything—his magic, his soul—to create a seal around the island. A prison. A warning.

The world survived. But it fractured.

The lands sealed themselves off. Travel was forbidden. Trust shattered. Peace became myth.

Present Day – Kiyoshi Aetherius

Fifteen years have passed.

The Land of the Present is quiet now—but not peaceful. Magic still exists, but it’s tightly controlled. People don’t talk about the meteors. Or the monsters. Or the Archons.

On the edge of a forest road, a boy walks alone.

His name is Kiyoshi Aetherius. Seventeen years old. Eyes like a storm. In his hand: a ring.

He doesn’t know why it whispers. He doesn’t know why dreams feel heavier. But something inside him has changed.

The wind stills. The sky darkens. And the ring begins to glow.

Kiyoshi doesn’t know it yet, but his blood carries the echo of the Archons.

And far, far away, on an island sealed for fifteen years… the barrier begins to crack.
